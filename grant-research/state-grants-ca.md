# California Small Business Grant Programs

## State Economic Development Agencies

### Governor's Office of Business and Economic Development (GO-Biz)
**Website:** business.ca.gov
**Focus:** Business attraction, retention, permitting assistance

| Program | Description | Benefit |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| California Competes Tax Credit | Job creation incentive | Negotiated tax credits |
| California Film & TV Tax Credit | Entertainment production | 20-25% tax credit |
| New Employment Credit | Hiring in designated areas | Tax credit per employee |
| California STEP | Export promotion | Subsidized trade events |

### California Office of the Small Business Advocate (CalOSBA)
**Website:** calosba.ca.gov
**Focus:** Small business advocacy, technical assistance coordination

| Program | Description | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Technical Assistance Program (TAP) | Training & consulting via centers | $23M annually statewide |
| Capital Infusion Program (CIP) | SBDC capital access support | $3M annually |
| Inclusive Innovation Hub | Underserved entrepreneur support | Varies |

**Note:** CalOSBA currently has no active direct-to-business grant programs. Sign up for their newsletter for new opportunities.

### California Infrastructure and Economic Development Bank (IBank)
**Website:** ibank.ca.gov
**Focus:** Infrastructure financing, small business lending

| Program | Description | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Small Business Loan Guarantee | Loan guarantees for capital access | Up to $5M (80% guarantee) |
| Venture Capital Access Program | VC investments in early-stage | Varies |
| Jump Start Loan Program | Microloans for underserved | $500-$10K |
| Small Business Disaster Loans | Post-disaster recovery | Up to $250K |

### California Pollution Control Financing Authority (CPCFA)
**Website:** treasurer.ca.gov/cpcfa
**Focus:** Environmental financing, small business credit support

| Program | Description | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| CalCAP for Small Business | Portfolio insurance for lenders | Up to 100% coverage |
| Collateral Support Program | Additional collateral support | Varies |
| SSBCI 2.0 Programs | Federal small business funding | Part of $1.1B allocation |

**SSBCI 2.0 Details:**
- California received $1.1B through American Rescue Plan Act
- Split between IBank and CPCFA
- Programs include loan guarantees, venture capital, collateral support
- Funds distributed in tranches through 2030

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Determine Eligibility
- California-based business
- Industry alignment with program
- Size standards (varies by program)
- Certification status if applicable

### Step 2: Get Certified (if applicable)
- **DGS Small Business/DVBE**: Certify.sbds.ca.gov
- **Federal SAM**: SAM.gov registration required for federal programs

### Step 3: Connect with SBDC
- Find your local SBDC: californiasbdc.org/find-your-sbdc/
- Free consulting on applications
- Market research assistance

### Step 4: Gather Documentation
- California business registration (Secretary of State)
- EIN and state tax ID
- Financial statements (2-3 years)
- Business plan
- California tax compliance (Franchise Tax Board)

### Step 5: Apply
- Most applications through online portals
- CalOSBA uses California Grants Portal: grants.ca.gov
- IBank applications through participating lenders

## Tips for CA Grant Success

1. **Use the California Grants Portal** - grants.ca.gov is centralized listing
2. **SBDC is your ally** - 56 centers, free consulting, application help
3. **Layer programs** - Combine IBank guarantees + local programs
4. **Get certified early** - SB/DVBE certification takes time but opens doors
5. **Focus on job creation** - Most CA programs tied to employment metrics
6. **Consider clean energy angle** - CEC and CPUC have substantial funding
7. **Check local first** - City/county programs often less competitive
8. **SBIR/STTR matching** - Some programs match federal awards
9. **Export programs underutilized** - CA STEP provides trade show support
10. **Timing matters** - TAP and CIP have annual cycles; watch deadlines

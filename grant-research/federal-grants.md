## SBIR/STTR Programs

### What's the Difference?
- **SBIR** - Small Business Innovation Research (independent R&D)
- **STTR** - Small Business Technology Transfer (requires research institution partner)

### Eligibility Requirements
- For-profit US business
- 51%+ US owned/controlled
- Principal investigator primarily employed by company
- SBIR: <500 employees
- STTR: <500 employees + nonprofit research partner

### Funding Phases
| Phase | Purpose | Typical Amount | Duration |
|-------|---------|----------------|----------|
| Phase I | Proof of concept | $50K-$275K | 6-12 months |
| Phase II | Prototype/R&D | $750K-$1.5M | 2 years |
| Phase III | Commercialization | No SBIR funds | Private/govt contracts |

### Top SBIR Agencies by Funding
1. DOD - $1.8B annually
2. HHS/NIH - $1.2B annually
3. DOE - $400M annually
4. NSF - $250M annually
5. NASA - $200M annually

### Application Tips
- Start with Phase I (lower barrier)
- Read agency-specific solicitations carefully
- Focus on commercialization potential
- Show technical feasibility AND market need
- Budget should match scope
- Prior SBIR awards help (but not required)

## SBA Direct Programs

### 7(a) Loan Program
- **Amount:** Up to $5 million
- **Use:** Working capital, equipment, real estate, refinancing
- **Terms:** Up to 25 years (real estate), 10 years (equipment)
- **Rates:** Prime + 2.25-4.75%
- **Guarantee:** 75-85% SBA backed

### 504 Loan Program
- **Amount:** Up to $5.5 million
- **Use:** Real estate, heavy equipment, major fixed assets
- **Structure:** 50% bank, 40% CDC, 10% borrower
- **Terms:** 10-25 years fixed rate

### Microloan Program
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000
- **Use:** Working capital, inventory, supplies, equipment
- **Terms:** Up to 6 years
- **Delivered through:** Nonprofit intermediaries

### Community Advantage
- **Amount:** Up to $350,000
- **Target:** Underserved markets
- **Delivered through:** Mission-focused lenders

### Disaster Loans
- **Business Physical:** Up to $2 million
- **EIDL:** Economic injury assistance
- **Trigger:** Declared disaster in area

## Economic Development Administration (EDA)

### EDA Programs
| Program | Focus | Typical Amount |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| Public Works | Infrastructure | $100K-$3M |
| Economic Adjustment | Diversification | $100K-$2M |
| Planning | Strategy development | $50K-$200K |
| Technical Assistance | Consulting | $50K-$150K |
| Build Back Better | Regional challenge | $25M-$75M |

### Eligibility
- Usually through state/local government or nonprofits
- Small businesses benefit indirectly
- Some direct business assistance in distressed areas

## USDA Rural Programs

### Business & Industry (B&I) Loan Guarantee
- **Amount:** Up to $25 million
- **Location:** Rural areas (<50K population)
- **Use:** Business development, equipment, real estate
- **Guarantee:** Up to 80%

### Rural Business Development Grants (RBDG)
- **Amount:** $10K-$500K
- **Target:** Rural small businesses
- **Use:** Training, technical assistance, planning

### Value-Added Producer Grants (VAPG)
- **Target:** Agricultural producers
- **Amount:** Up to $250K
- **Use:** Value-added processing

## Registration Requirements

### SAM.gov (Required for Federal Grants)
1. Get UEI number (replaced DUNS)
2. Register at SAM.gov
3. Renew annually
4. Takes 7-10 business days
5. FREE (beware scam sites)

### Grants.gov Account
1. Create account
2. Link to SAM.gov
3. Authorize representatives
4. Download Workspace (for applications)

### SBIR/STTR Registration
1. SBA Company Registry
2. Agency-specific portals (Research.gov, NSPIRES, etc.)

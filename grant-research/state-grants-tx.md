# Texas Small Business Grant Programs

## State Economic Development Agencies

### Office of the Texas Governor - Economic Development & Tourism
**Website:** gov.texas.gov/business
**Focus:** Business attraction, incentives, workforce development

| Program | Description | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Texas Enterprise Fund (TEF) | Deal-closing incentive for major projects | $250K-$50M+ |
| Texas Enterprise Zone Program (EZP) | Sales tax refunds in distressed areas | $625K-$3.75M |
| Skills for Small Business | Training grants via TWC | Up to $1,450/new employee |
| Product Development Fund (PDSBI) | Loans for product development | Varies |

**Texas Enterprise Fund Details:**
- Largest "deal-closing" fund in the nation
- Requires 75+ new jobs (urban) or 25+ (rural)
- Average wage must exceed county average
- Discretionary awards based on economic impact

**Enterprise Zone Program Details:**
- Communities nominate companies
- Tax refunds range $2,500-$7,500 per qualified job
- Must be in economically distressed area
- Investment and job creation thresholds required

### Texas Economic Development Finance
**Website:** gov.texas.gov/business/page/financing-and-capital
**Focus:** Cost-effective financing tools

| Program | Description | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Product Development & Small Business Incubator (PDSBI) | Long-term asset-backed loans | $20M program total |
| Capital Access Program | Portfolio insurance for lenders | Varies |
| Industrial Revenue Bonds | Tax-exempt bond financing | Large projects |

**PDSBI Details:**
- For product development companies and incubators
- Requires 3 years operating history
- Preferred: semiconductors, nanotech, biotech, biomedicine
- Interest rates: fixed + variable components

### Texas Workforce Commission (TWC)
**Website:** twc.texas.gov
**Focus:** Workforce development, training grants

| Program | Description | Amount |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Skills for Small Business | Training for small business employees | Up to $2M annually statewide |
| Skills Development Fund | Customized job training | Up to $500K per project |
| Self-Sufficiency Fund | Training for TANF recipients | Varies |
| Apprenticeship Program | Registered apprenticeships | Tax credits + funding |

**Skills for Small Business:**
- Businesses with <100 employees
- Up to $1,450 per new full-time employee
- Up to $725 per existing employee
- Training through community colleges

## Demographic-Specific Programs

### CRITICAL UPDATE: HUB Program Changes (December 2025)

**The Historically Underutilized Business (HUB) program has undergone major changes:**
- Women and minority-owned businesses **removed** from program
- Program renamed to **Veteran Heroes United in Business (VetHUB)**
- Only service-disabled veterans (20%+ disability) now eligible
- Changes effective immediately under emergency rules
- Previously certified MBE/WBE businesses had certifications revoked

### Veteran-Owned Businesses (Current Focus)
- **VetHUB Program** (formerly HUB)
  - 20%+ service-connected disability required
  - State contracting preference
  - Contact: Texas Comptroller's Office

- **Texas Veterans Commission**
  - Business resources
  - Website: tvc.texas.gov

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Determine Eligibility
- Texas-based business
- Industry alignment
- Size requirements (varies)
- Current certification status

### Step 2: Connect with SBDC
- Find your local SBDC
- Free consulting and application help
- Website: sbdctexas.org

### Step 3: Check Local Programs
- City/county programs often available
- Less competitive than state programs
- Check your municipality's economic development office

### Step 4: Gather Documentation
- Texas business registration (Secretary of State)
- EIN
- Financial statements (2-3 years)
- Business plan
- Texas franchise tax compliance

### Step 5: Apply
- State programs: gov.texas.gov
- Local programs: Contact city/county directly
- TWC programs: twc.texas.gov

## Tips for TX Grant Success

1. **Check local FIRST** - City programs less competitive, still operating MBE/WBE
2. **SBDC is essential** - 40+ centers, completely free consulting
3. **Focus on job creation** - TEF and EZP are job-driven
4. **Skills grants are underused** - $1,450/employee through TWC
5. **Rural advantage** - Lower thresholds for rural projects
6. **H-E-B Quest** - Great for consumer products, high visibility
7. **International trade** - UTSA Trade Center helps with exports
8. **Government contracting** - PTAC/CGC helps with federal contracts
9. **Veterans prioritized** - VetHUB is now primary state program
10. **Watch for legislative changes** - HUB changes may be challenged; monitor updates
